🎯 ¿Qué es Lumina Score para Creativos?

Lumina Score es tu mejor brújula. Te dice qué ideas realmente conectan con los usuarios, midiendo tanto la atracción (IPM) como el valor (ROAS). Es la forma de saber si tu creatividad no solo gusta, sino que también funciona.

Conclusión clave: Un Lumina Score alto significa que tu visión creativa y los objetivos de negocio van de la mano. ¡Es la prueba de que tu idea es un éxito!

❌ Bottom Left Quadrant

Low IPM + Low ROAS D0

Underperforming on all metrics.

UA Actions:
  • Evaluate active campaign presence
  • Calculate opportunity cost of continuation
  • If high spend (large bubble), phase out strategically
  • Share failure patterns with creative team

🔧 Bottom Right Quadrant

High IPM + Low ROAS D0

Volume drivers with monetization challenges.

UA Actions:
  • Test IAP campaigns
  • Mix with high-ROAS creatives (50/50 split)
  • Coordinate iteration with creative team
  • Test on low volume install countries (Nordics for instance)

📈 Advanced Scaling Insights

IPM-CPI Relationship:

  • High IPM → Network finds users easily → Lower competition → Lower CPI
  • IPM is creative-intrinsic; CPI is market-dependent
  • Use IPM for creative evaluation, CPI for budget planning

Scaling Probability Factors:

High IPM + Good ROAS

Maximum scaling potential. Networks should recognize and prioritize.

Low IPM + High ROAS

Scale ceiling exists. Audience quality high but limited reach.

High IPM + Low ROAS

Network hesitation. Manual optimization required.

Key Takeaway: Lumina Score already weights for statistical significance and environment normalization. Trust the score for decision-making, but always consider context.

💡 Maximizing Success Likelihood

The Success Multiplier Framework:

🎲 Probability Boosters

Each action increases success probability by ~20%:

  1. Pre-launch Creative Review: Growth provides placement context before creative production
  2. Hook Testing: Test 3 different hooks for winning concepts
  3. Audience Matching: Creative briefs include target audience data
  4. Format Optimization: Create platform-specific versions (9:16, 1:1, 16:9)
  5. Rapid Iteration: 48-hour turnaround on performance-based tweaks

Common Pitfalls & Solutions:

❌ Pitfall: "This creative should work"

✓ Solution: Let data guide decisions. If it's bottom-left for 2 weeks, pivot regardless of initial excitement.

❌ Pitfall: "Just run it everywhere"

✓ Solution: Match creative style to placement. Social-first creatives need social placements.

❌ Pitfall: "The data must be wrong"

✓ Solution: Dashboard aggregates fairly. If scores are low, focus on understanding why rather than questioning the data.

❌ Pitfall: "Let's change everything"

✓ Solution: Iterate one element at a time. Change hook OR message OR visual, not all three.

💡 Game-Changing Insights Most Teams Miss

The 80/20 Rule of Creative Performance

• 80% of your revenue will come from 20% of concepts (top right quadrant)

• BUT: 80% of your install volume might come from bottom right quadrant

• Strategy: Balance portfolio - need both for healthy growth

The Platform Psychology Factor

• Social platforms (FB/IG): Respond to emotional hooks and social proof

• SDK networks: Respond to gameplay and value demonstrations

• Implication: Same creative, different performance by platform is NORMAL

The Scaling Momentum Window

• Week 1-2: Creative in "honeymoon phase" - best performance

• Week 3-4: Natural decline begins - need variations ready

• Best practice: Launch variations when original hits 70% of peak IPM

💬 Communication Templates

When Creative has performance doubts:

Instead of: Wondering silently or making assumptions

Do this: Screenshot the dashboard, share the link in Slack: "Hey Growth team, I'm seeing [specific metric] for our [concept name]. Can you help me understand what placements we're running and why performance might be [specific observation]?"

💡 Tip: Always include dashboard screenshot + link for context

When Growth needs iterations that scale:

Instead of: "We need to lower CPI" (this encourages misleading creatives)

Do this: "Creative team, [concept X] shows strong performance in [placement] with [specific metric]. Dashboard shows [screenshot] potential to scale if we can improve [specific element]. Can we test 2-3 variations focusing on [specific request based on data]?"

💡 Tip: Justify requests with market data to align on quality users, not just cheap installs

When celebrating success:

Always: Share dashboard screenshot showing the winning creative's journey: "This win happened because Creative nailed [specific element] and Growth found the perfect [placement/audience]. Here's what we learned for next time..."

💡 Tip: Tag both teams and save insights for future reference

When addressing underperformance:

Instead of: "This creative isn't working"

Do this: "Dashboard data [screenshot] shows [concept] in bottom left quadrant after [timeframe]. Let's analyze: Creative team - any hypothesis on messaging? Growth team - which audiences/placements did we test? Together - pivot or iterate?"
